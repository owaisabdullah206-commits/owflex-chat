Most freelancers underprice AI chatbots because they price the tool, not the outcome.

Wrong question: "What does this chatbot cost me?"
Right question: "What is this chatbot worth to my client?"

Here's how I think about it:

A clinic in Karachi gets 30 calls a day asking: "What are your hours?" "Do you accept XYZ insurance?" "How do I book?"

That's 30 calls their receptionist answers manually.

An AI chatbot handles all 30.
24 hours a day.
Including weekends.

The clinic saves maybe 2 hours of staff time per day.
At even ₨200/hour that's ₨120,000 a month in recovered time.

You are charging ₨10,000 to ₨15,000/month.
You are keeping ₨7,500 after tool costs.
The clinic is saving ₨120,000.

That is not a tech purchase. That is a business decision.

When you frame it that way in a proposal, the conversation changes.

Stop selling software.
Start selling the outcome the software creates.

How do you currently price services for clients?
