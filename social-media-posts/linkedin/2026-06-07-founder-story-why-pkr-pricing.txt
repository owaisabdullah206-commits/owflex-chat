Every tool I researched to build my product charged in dollars.

$197/month. $220/month. $360/month.

That is ₨55,000 to ₨100,000 a month.

The freelancers I am building for charge ₨30,000 to ₨50,000 for a full website project.
The tool would cost more than the project.

So when I set pricing for my product, I did not think too long about it.

Starter plan: ₨2,500/month.

The freelancer charges their client ₨10,000/month for the service.
They keep ₨7,500.
The tool does not eat the margin. That is the whole point.

I am sharing this because a lot of people building in Pakistan default to dollar pricing.
Sometimes it makes sense. Sometimes you are just copying what you see.

If your customer earns in rupees and pays bills in rupees, price in rupees.
It took me 30 seconds to decide. It should have been obvious from day one.

What pricing decisions have you made early in a project that you later realized were obvious?
