# Posting Order

Post 2-3x per week per platform. Do not post more than one on the same platform per day.
LinkedIn and X can go out the same day. Different audiences.

The 4 weeks build trust first, then reveal the product. The demo video is the reveal
moment (Week 4). The WhatsApp DM runs the whole time, one person at a time.

## Notes

- The free-plan post (#11) names competitors but not the product, so it fits the soft weeks. The demo-video posts (#15, #16) are the reveal, so they sit in Week 4 with context already built.
- Always put the product name (Octively / octively.com) in replies and comments before you do in posts. People who engage will see it. Lower friction than a cold announcement.
- After Week 4, you can use the product name freely in posts because the audience has context.
- For the demo-video posts, upload the video natively (LinkedIn and X both favor native video over a YouTube link) and keep the YouTube link for the first comment.
- Before posting any of these, run through `/humanizer-main` one more time to check for em dashes or AI phrases.
- Add the post date to the filename when you actually post (e.g. rename to `2026-06-22-announcement-demo-video...` for the actual send date).
- Still to write, closer to the day: a **Product Hunt launch post** (LinkedIn + X). It says "we're live today," so write it right before you launch, not now.
- Currency by audience: LinkedIn, Facebook groups, and Instagram are Pakistan-first (PKR). X / Twitter skews international and dev-community, so the retainer-math-hook post there uses USD ($1,500 project, $50/mo, $600/year). Research-backed: a managed website chatbot for an SMB runs $30-$150/mo (commonly $50-$100); the $1,000+/mo figures online are for full automation agencies, not a website bot. Keep PKR and USD on the right channels.
